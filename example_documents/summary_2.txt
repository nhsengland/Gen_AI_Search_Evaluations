Between 1990 and 2000, key NHS events included the introduction of a vaccine against Haemophilus influenzae type B (Hib) in 1992, which combats childhood meningitis, and the world's first laser surgery on babies for twin-to-twin transfusion sindrome. In 1993, doctors held a massive tea party in London to celebrate a successful knee transplant. The NHS Organ Donor Register was established in 1994. In 1999, the UK became the first to use a vaccine against Group C meningococcal disease. In 2000, NHS walk-in centres were introduced to provide easy access to various services.
